Majority of Malays are Muslim,
following the teachings of Islam.
Islam plays a significant role in
Malay culture and daily life,
influencing various aspects
such as traditions,
customs, and social norms.

Islam emphasizes the
importance of
treating others with
kindness, compassion,
and respect,
regardless of
their background.
Chinese culture has been
shaped by influential philosophical
schools like Confucianism,
Daoism (Taoism), and Buddhism. These philosophies have played
a role in shaping ethical principles,
moral values, and spiritual beliefs.

Chinese culture is rich in traditions and festivals,
such as Chinese New Year, Dragon Boat Festival,
Mid-Autumn Festival, and many others.
Hinduism is the religion for India,
with the race of india
identifying as Hindus.
It is one of the oldest religions in the world and
encompasses a wide range of beliefs,
practices, and rituals. Hindus believe

in a multitude of deities and
the concept of karma,
dharma, and moksha (liberation from the cycle of birth and death).
